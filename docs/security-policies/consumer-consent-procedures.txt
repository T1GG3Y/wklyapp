## 1. Purpose

This document describes how Wkly ("the Company") obtains, records, and manages consumer consent for the collection, processing, and storage of their data, particularly financial data accessed through Plaid.

## 2. Consent Principles

### 2.1 Informed Consent

Consumers are provided with clear, understandable information about:
- What data is collected
- Why data is collected
- How data is used
- Who data is shared with
- How long data is retained
- Their rights regarding their data

### 2.2 Freely Given

- Consent is not a condition for basic service access
- Consumers can withdraw consent at any time
- No penalty for withdrawing consent beyond loss of related features

### 2.3 Specific

- Consent is obtained for specific purposes
- Separate consent for materially different uses
- No bundled consent that obscures specific uses

### 2.4 Unambiguous

- Clear affirmative action required (not pre-checked boxes)
- Plain language, not legal jargon
- Consent mechanism clearly distinguishable

## 3. Consent Collection Points

### 3.1 Account Registration

**When:** Consumer creates an account

**Consent Obtained For:**
- Collection of account information (name, email, phone)
- Processing for service provision
- Communication for service updates and support

**Mechanism:**
- Privacy Policy link displayed
- Checkbox: "I have read and agree to the [Privacy Policy]"
- Account cannot be created without affirmative consent

**Record Captured:**
- Timestamp
- IP address
- Version of Privacy Policy accepted
- Device information

### 3.2 Financial Account Connection (Plaid Link)

**When:** Consumer connects a financial institution through Plaid Link

**Consent Obtained For:**
- Access to financial account information
- Access to transaction history
- Ongoing data sync for connected accounts
- Data sharing between Plaid and Wkly

**Mechanism:**
1. **Pre-Link Disclosure:**
   - Clear explanation of what data will be accessed
   - Purpose of data access explained
   - Link to full Privacy Policy

2. **Plaid Link Consent:**
   - Consumer authenticates with financial institution
   - Plaid's consent screen explains data access
   - Consumer explicitly authorizes connection

3. **Post-Link Confirmation:**
   - Confirmation screen showing connected accounts
   - Reminder of data usage
   - Option to disconnect at any time

**Record Captured:**
- Timestamp of connection
- Institution connected
- Account types connected
- Consent version
- Plaid consent token reference

### 3.4 Marketing Communications (If Applicable)

**When:** Consumer opts in to marketing communications

**Consent Obtained For:**
- Promotional emails
- Product updates and announcements

**Mechanism:**
- Separate, unchecked checkbox
- Clear description of communication types
- Not required for service

**Record Captured:**
- Timestamp
- Communication types selected
- Consent version

## 4. Consent Management

### 4.1 Consumer Access to Consent

Consumers can view and manage their consent through:
- **Settings > Privacy** in the App
- Requesting consent records via email

### 4.2 Modifying Consent

Consumers can:
- Disconnect financial accounts (revokes Plaid data access)
- Opt out of marketing communications
- Request deletion of data collected under consent

### 4.3 Withdrawing Consent

**Process:**
1. Consumer initiates withdrawal (app settings or written request)
2. Company acknowledges within 48 hours
3. Consent-dependent processing stops immediately
4. Related data deleted per retention policy
5. Confirmation sent to consumer

**Impact:**
- Disconnecting financial accounts removes linked account features
- Full consent withdrawal may require account deletion

## 5. Consent for Minors

- Service is not intended for users under 18
- Age verification at registration
- Accounts of users discovered to be under 18 are suspended

## 6. Record Keeping

### 6.1 Consent Records

For each consent, the following is recorded and retained:
- Consumer identifier
- Timestamp (UTC)
- Type of consent
- Version of applicable policy/disclosure
- Method of consent (checkbox, button click, etc.)
- IP address and device information
- Any modifications or withdrawals

### 6.2 Retention

Consent records are retained for:
- Duration of account relationship
- Plus 7 years after account closure (for legal compliance)

### 6.3 Audit Trail

Consent records are:
- Immutable (append-only modifications)
- Available for regulatory audit
- Backed up securely

## 7. Re-Consent Requirements

Re-consent is required when:
- Material changes to Privacy Policy
- New data uses not covered by original consent
- Regulatory requirements change
- Significant changes to Plaid integration

**Re-Consent Process:**
1. Consumer notified of changes
2. Updated disclosure presented
3. Affirmative consent required to continue
4. Grace period for review (if applicable)
5. Service limited if consent not renewed

## 8. Third-Party Consent (Plaid)

### 8.1 Plaid's Role

Plaid acts as an intermediary for financial data:
- Plaid obtains direct consent through Plaid Link
- Plaid maintains its own consent records
- Consumer can revoke Plaid access through financial institution

### 8.2 Our Obligations

We ensure:
- Plaid Link consent flow is not modified
- Consumer sees Plaid's consent disclosures
- We honor Plaid's consent requirements
- We respond to Plaid consent revocation signals

## 9. Compliance

This consent framework is designed to comply with:
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- Other U.S. state privacy laws
- Plaid's developer requirements
- Industry best practices
